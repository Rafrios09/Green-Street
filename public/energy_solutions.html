Solar Energy

Illuminating the Future with Solar Power

Solar Power. One of the first things that comes to many minds when the topic of renewable energy comes up. Harnessing the sun’s powerful energy through a system of mirrors and panels, Solar panels convert sunlight into electricity using photovoltaic cells or concentrating solar power systems.

Facts

  • Solar energy is a renewable energy source that can significantly reduce a home's electricity bill. For instance, the most common form of solar utilize solar panels are found on the roofs of houses.
  • Solar panels gather sunlight, which is subsequently turned into direct current (DC) power via inverters. The electricity generated may subsequently be utilized to power household appliances and lights. The surplus electricity can then be sent back to the grid as net metering credits. Some solar systems contain batteries, which may store extra electricity for later use. Homeowners may also utilize a monitoring device to measure their solar energy output and savings. Overall, solar energy systems are a clean and efficient source of energy, decreasing dependency on traditional sources and perhaps lowering long-term energy expenses.

Hydroelectric Energy

Flowing Energy, Powering Progress

Dating back hundreds of generations, Hydropower utilizes the flow of water to generate mechanical power. The simplified concept of hydropower involves harnessing the energy from flowing water to turn gears, turbines, or wheels and convert kinetic energy into mechanical energy.

Facts

  • Based on the geographical location of businesses homeowners can utilize the natural flows of waterways, such as rivers or lakes, making it a clean and sustainable option for electricity. Furthermore, in states such as Washington and Oregon that primarily utilize Hydropower, the cost-to-electricity ratio is significantly cheaper than in traditional sources.
  • Accounting for 28.7% of total U.S. renewable electricity generation and about 6.2% of total U.S. electricity generation.
  • One simple form of hydropower that may appeal to many average consumers is the Micro Hydropower System. Generating up to 100 kilowatts of electricity, these simplified forms of hydropower are commonly utilized by homeowners and small enterprises such as farmers or personal projects. The systematic components of a Microhydropower System include: Water Conveyance - Any form of piping that helps transport water Mechanized turbine, pump, or waterwheel - Converts the water’s kinetic energy into mechanical energy Alternator or generator - Generates electricity from the mechanical energy

Wind Energy

Harnessing the Power of the Breeze

Picture yourself on a long road trip. You look at the horizon and see large spinning towers resembling a gigantic fan. These towering structures are powered by wind energy. Wind energy is considered to be one of many primary clean renewable energy sources across the world. Relying on only nature, wind energy creates a variety of beneficial outcomes for both the environment and consumers alike.

Facts

  • One of the most common techniques used to harness wind energy is through the utilization of large wind turbines that use kinetic energy from the wind and turn it into electrical power. Wind turbines especially generate energy at significantly reduced costs and as the wind energy industry experiences ongoing expansion, scientific advancements contribute to substantial reductions in production and maintenance costs.
  • These spinning towers, harnessing the wind's kinetic energy, exemplify a change towards sustainable power generation. According to the Department of Energy, wind energy sources across the United States reduce around 336 million metric tons of carbon dioxide emissions per year, or the equivalent of the emission of 73 million cars on the road. Additionally, unlike finite fossil fuels, wind is an abundant and perpetual resource, ensuring a continuous and eco-friendly energy supply.
  • Wind power benefits local communities. Homes and businesses can reduce their electricity expenses by 50–90% with small wind electric systems, especially in remote places where connecting to utility power lines is economically counterintuitive.

Geothermal Energy

Geothermal Innovation, Grounded in Sustainability

Geothermal energy is thermal heat energy that radiates from the inner Earth. This thermal heat is then used to generate other forms of useable energy such as electricity, heating, and other various applications for human utilization. One conventional method of geothermal energy is in the form of Geothermal reservoirs. Acting as a deep system of wells, geothermal resources allow heat and trapped high water temperatures to be brought up to the surface level as an energy resource.

Facts

  • Geothermal Heating/cooling provides a variety of benefits for new active homeowners in search of an efficient and renewable energy source. From an economic standpoint, geothermal temperature control in a house can significantly increase a homeowner’s property value. In addition, thanks to its reliability and low operating costs, geothermal temperature control systems are a substantial economic investment in the long run as there are few repairs and reduced bills.
  • A typical homeowner interested in installing a conventional geothermal system should expect initial pricing to be around $50,000 or more. However, as society focuses on more sustainable energy sources, geothermal energy is gaining attention as a reliable and continuous renewable energy option. As technology and research expand in this sector of renewable energy, factors of efficiency and lower costs will increase as well.
